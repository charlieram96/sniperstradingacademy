Charts

The four chart forms the system draws — sparkline, area, bar and donut — plus the card they live in and the rules that keep them honest. Gold is the lead series; supporting series step through the chart palette. Gridlines stay at --border-subtle, and every stroke and endpoint dot is computed to sit inside its viewBox.

Sparkline

120 × 36 · 4px right margin
viewBox 120 × 36 · plot ends at x=116 · 4px right margin
endpoint dot Ø6 reaches x=119.75 — inside the frame
1Fixed viewBox — 120 × 36, no axes or labels. A sparkline is a shape, not a chart; it rides inline in stat tiles.
2Right-margin rule — the line stops at x=116 so the 4px gap holds the endpoint dot; the dot never clips the frame.
3Endpoint dot — Ø6 gold marker on the last point, ringed in --bg so it reads against the fill.
4Round caps — 1.5px stroke, round linecap and linejoin; the gold gradient fades to zero at the baseline.

06

Do & don't

flat marks · ≤5 series · subtle gridlines
Don't
  • No 3D, extrusion or drop-shadowed marks — depth distorts the values.
  • Never exceed 5 series — the palette stops at --chart-5 for a reason.
  • No heavy gridlines competing with the data ink.
Do
  • Flat marks on a single plane — the height is the value.
  • Up to 5 series straight from the chart palette.
  • Gridlines held at --border-subtle; the baseline one step up at --border.
